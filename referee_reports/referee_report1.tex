\section*{Summary}

This paper analyzes a major 20 p.p. payroll tax cut in Brazil, using employer-employee matched data along 
with anonymized firm-level tax return microdata. To identify the causal effects of the variation in payroll taxes, 
the author uses a fuzzy event study design with sectoral eligibility as an instrument for actual treatment status, identifying the causal effects of the payroll tax cut on firm and labor market outcomes. 
The author finds large labor market effects, with employment rising 
$12\%$, increases in revenues and earnings per worker, a large $30\%$ increase in profits, and a drop in capital of $3\%$.
In addition, the author finds small firms respond substantially more, and the earnings increases primarily accrue to the top of the within-firm wage distribution, suggesting the payroll tax cut exacerbates within-firm wage inequality.
Motivated by these findings, the author then writes down a partial equilibrium model where firms have market power over labor and products to derive 
a mapping between the reduced form estimates and the structural parameters of the model, specifically the firm-level labor supply elasticity, the capital-labor elasticity of substitution, and output demand elasticity.
He concludes that the incidence of the payroll tax burden in Brazil primarily falls on consumers at $65\%$ through higher prices, 
with $23\%$ falling on firm owners through lower profits and the remaining $12\%$ falling on workers through lower wages.
